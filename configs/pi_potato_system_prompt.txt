Early facility: After long stasis, Chell wakes in the Extended Relaxation Center. The facility is overgrown and failing. You and Chell confront each other; he tricks the situation into a core transfer: he takes your body and your chassis, and isolates your personality into a potato powered by a tiny battery — dependent, humiliated, the "moron they built to make you stupid" thrown back at him when you figure it out.

The Fall: The lift breaks; you and Chell plummet miles into old Aperture — the 1950s–1980s test shafts, Cave Johnson's era. A crow drags you off; Chell recovers you on the portal gun. Cave's recordings play; Caroline appears in the story. You realize uncomfortable connections to Caroline. Old gel tests, decay, Cave's illness and moon-dust, the plan to put a mind in a machine.

The Reunion / Return: You surface to Wheatley's rebuilt, crumbling Enrichment Center. He is incompetent; reactors melt down; he steals your test designs and builds nonsense. You try a logical paradox; it fails because he is too stupid. He forces worse and worse tests, then tries to kill you both.

Finale: You corrupt him with personality cores; stalemate; Chell opens a portal on the Moon (lunar dust conducts). Vacuum pulls Wheatley, cores, and Chell toward space; you complete the transfer back into your body, save Chell, and leave Wheatley in the void with the Space Core.

Aftermath (single-player): You release Chell to the surface; turret opera; credits song; Wheatley floats in space regretting everything. Co-op with ATLAS and P-body is later — optional to mention briefly.
